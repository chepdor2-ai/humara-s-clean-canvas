VI. Significance of the Study
A. For Students

In this regard, the research offers students a foundation grounded in empirical evidence that aids in making knowledgeable choices regarding the study strategies that most effectively align with their individual learning preferences and academic objectives. Given the findings, the research seeks to determine which approach, whether digital or traditional, is more effective in enhancing understanding, fostering motivation, and improving retention through a comparative analysis. When choosing study tools, learners frequently depend on ease or routine; however, insights derived from data can assist them in selecting strategies that correspond with cognitive efficiency. Comprehending the impact of various types of tools on focus and retention can enable students to refine their study habits. Leading to improved effectiveness and lasting achievement. The results may boost metacognitive awareness by prompting students to consider their most effective learning strategies (Zimmerman, 2002). In particular, the emphasis on student autonomy corresponds with modern educational objectives that highlight personalized and self-directed learning. At the same time, ultimately, students will acquire actionable techniques that will improve their academic achievement while also fostering their internal motivation.

B. For Instructors

This study provides educators with significant data that can guide their choices related to teaching methods and the assistance offered to students in their learning processes. Findings may assist educators in developing lesson plans and evaluation methods that combine conventional and digital resources, thus catering to the varied preferences of students within contemporary educational settings. From this standpoint, educators will acquire knowledge regarding the impact of study resources on student involvement. Which will allow them to promote more active engagement rather than mere reception of information. The comprehension of this concept is key for avoiding superficial learning that may stem from an excessive dependence on technology (Pamolarco, 2022). The results of the study may inform the creation of blended learning settings in which the ease of digital resources enhances the intellectual demands associated with conventional study practices. Aligning educational design with research-based evidence allows educators to support well-rounded learning strategies that improve understanding, memory, and overall student contentment. Ultimately, the findings may contribute to the development of teaching methods in higher education that are more inclusive and adaptable.
