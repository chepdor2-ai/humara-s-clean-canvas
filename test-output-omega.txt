B. For Instructors

With this in mind, the findings from this study provide significant data that educators can use to guide their decisions related to teaching methods and the support offered to students in their learning processes. In practical terms, findings may assist educators in developing instructional strategies and evaluation methods that incorporate both conventional and digital resources; thus catering to the varied learning preferences of students in contemporary educational settings. From this standpoint, educators will acquire understanding regarding the impact of study resources on student involvement, though the specifics vary by context. Allowing for the promotion of more active participation instead of mere information absorption. A thorough comprehension of this issue matters for averting superficial learning, which may arise from excessive dependence on technology (pamolarco, 2022). Study may's findings assist in shaping blended learning settings in which the ease of digital tools enhances the intellectual discipline associated with conventional study practices. Aligning instructional design with research-supported findings enables educators to advance well-rounded learning methods that improve understanding, memory retention, and overall student contentment. Put it all together, and the understanding derived from this analysis may feed into the development of teaching methods in higher education that are both more inclusive and adaptable.
